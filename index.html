Introduction
About the Theme

Knowledge is Power.

Our theme, "Every Child, Every Right," is drawn directly from the UN Convention on the Rights of a Child (UNCRC). We believe that for a child to exercise their rights, they must first know them.

Why UNCRC?

By framing our academic competitions around these rights, we transform a "Spelling Bee" or a "Coding Challenge" into an act of self-expression (Article 13) and a celebration of the right to develop talents to the full (Article 29).

Teacher Guidelines

Empowering Voices.

How to facilitate a festival that respects the English-medium context while honoring the students' diverse backgrounds.

Diverse Perspectives

While the contests are held in English, encourage students to bring their unique cultural ideas and family histories into their writing and speeches.

Excellence

We celebrate the Excellence and Achievement Every attempt is a valuable part of the learning journey. Our top honours distinguish work that demonstrates superior skill and a comprehensive fulfillment of the competition rubrics.

Safe Environment

Homeroom teachers act as coaches. Focus on preparation and guidance to ensure every representative feels confident on stage.

Inclusive Action

Prepare light, independent enrichment activities (scribbling, pattern drawing) for non-participating pupils so the whole class stays engaged.
